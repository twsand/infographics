The "Big Beautiful Bill"

What they promised working Americans vs. what's actually in the law

The Headline Promise

"No Tax on Tips. No Tax on Overtime."

What They Said
"No taxes on your tips. No taxes on your overtime. Period."
What The Law Says
A capped tax deduction with income limits that expires in 2028.

For a tax preparer, this distinction is everything. This isn't an exclusion from gross income‚Äîit's an above-the-line deduction under IRC ¬ß¬ß 224-225. That means:

1

You Still Pay Payroll Taxes

7.65% FICA taxes still apply to every dollar of tips and overtime. That never changed. For a server making $40,000 with $20,000 in tips, that's still $1,530 in payroll tax on tips alone.

2

It's Capped

Tips: $25,000 max deduction (not per spouse‚Äî$25k total for married couples). Overtime: Only $12,500 for single filers. And only the premium qualifies‚Äîthe "half" in time-and-a-half, not the full overtime rate.

3

Income Phaseouts

Benefits start phasing out at $150,000 MAGI (single) / $300,000 (joint). Phases out by $100 for every $1,000 over the threshold.

4

37% of Tipped Workers Get Nothing

According to Yale Budget Lab, 37% of tipped workers already paid zero federal income tax. A deduction gives you nothing when your tax liability is already zero.

5

It Expires December 31, 2028

Conveniently, right when the current presidential term ends. It's a campaign deliverable, not permanent policy.
